# Dataset Statement

## What Is Real

The MVP includes real Zimbabwean tourism places, including Victoria Falls, Lake Kariba, Nyanga, Great Zimbabwe, Mana Pools, Matobo, Hwange, Chinhoyi Caves, Domboshava, Chiremba Balancing Rocks and other destinations. Destination categories, regions, common activities, travel themes and safety reminders are curated to reflect realistic tourism use cases.

## What Is Estimated or Simulated

Budget figures are planning estimates, not official prices. Entrance fees, activity costs, accommodation assumptions, transport estimates and group discounts are approximate MVP values intended to demonstrate budgeting logic. They must be validated with official tourism authorities, accommodation providers, activity operators and transport partners before production launch.

Example memories, traveller voices and some demonstration imagery are illustrative placeholders used to show the intended product experience.

## Data Still Needed

- Official and regularly updated entrance fees.
- Verified accommodation availability and pricing.
- Licensed taxi, shuttle and transfer provider data.
- Activity operator prices and safety requirements.
- Official tourism destination metadata and opening times.
- Production-ready image library with recorded usage rights.
- User testing evidence from travellers, schools, local tourists and tourism operators.

## Image Licensing Caveat

The MVP contains a mixture of project-generated, user-provided and curated images. For judging and prototype demonstration this is acceptable as product evidence, but commercial launch should use only images that are owned, licensed, partner-provided or generated with clear usage rights.

## AI and Dataset Boundary

Nyika AI currently uses local curated tourism data, synonym lists, intent parsing, matching rules and budget profiles. It does not depend on third-party LLM calls. Future model integration should use retrieval over verified Zimbabwe tourism datasets and should retain transparent budget calculations outside the model.
